Technical Support - SnoreBox

Frequently Asked Questions

Why must I allow microphone access?

The core feature of SnoreBox is the detection and analysis of sleep audio. In order to capture and intelligently identify your snoring sounds during the night, the application must acquire access to your device's microphone.

⚠️ Privacy Commitment: We take your privacy extremely seriously. All your recordings, analysis data, and sleep reports are exclusively kept locally on your device, and will never be uploaded to any cloud or third-party servers. Please feel completely assured to use it.

How to ensure normal recording throughout the night?

To acquire the most accurate sleep and snoring analysis report, we recommend following these steps:

  1. Place the phone in a stable location near the head of the bed (within 1 meter of your head).
  2. Ensure the microphone at the bottom of the phone is not covered by pillows, blankets, or books.
  3. It is recommended to connect the phone to power during recording.

Why did I find the recording stopped partway through when I woke up?

If the recording failed to cover the entire night, it is usually caused by system mechanisms. Please check:

  • Whether the device's battery was too low, triggering the iOS "Low Power Mode" which forcefully suspends background activities.
  • Whether other apps (such as alarms, incoming calls, WeChat voice calls, or playing music) interrupted the system's audio session during the night.

How can I export my snoring recordings?

  1. Open the app and go to "History".
  2. Select the detailed sleep report for a certain date.
  3. In the list of audio clips, tap the "Share" or "Export" button next to a specific recording.
  4. You can save it to the system's "Files" app or send it to your health consultant.

Troubleshooting

No sound is heard during playback

If there is no sound when you play a captured snoring clip, please check:

  • Whether your device is in "Silent Mode" (please try turning up the side volume button to increase media volume).
  • Whether a Bluetooth headset or speaker is connected, causing the sound to be transmitted to an external device.

App crashes or becomes unresponsive

  1. Swipe up in the multitasking interface to force quit SnoreBox.
  2. Reopen the app.

If the problem persists, please provide feedback to us via the contact information below.
